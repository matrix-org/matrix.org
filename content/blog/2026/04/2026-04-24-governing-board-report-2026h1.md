Following a packed first half of the year, the Board came together last week
for it's first-half Board meeting. A lot has been going on - in the world, in
software, and in Matrix, and it's clear things are not slowing down. So what
have we been discussing?

<!-- more -->

## Working groups continue to drive things

When we founded the board, we put the ability for the community to be involved
at the heart of things. The [Working Group
process](@/foundation/governing-board/bylaws/04-processes.md) was highlighted
at The Matrix Conference, and that has now lead to new WGs self-organising,
getting things done, and reaching out to the Board for official status.

This is a big deal, as it shows the system is now up and running, raises
awareness of how others can follow the same path, makes more WG members aware
of what the Board does (and why they might want to be on it), and so on. It's
obviously great to see more work getting done in more places (and wow, there's
still a lot to do) - and remember, if you have an idea for something *else* to
get done, you can [propose a WG
yourself](@/foundation/governing-board/bylaws/02-bylaws.md#5-working-groups)! 

## Finance and sustainability

While all the Committees and their associated working groups have been busy
(see below), another was the Finance Committee coming to life. This has been
the hardest to get rolling (understandably - it's complex and invisible work),
but with both Amandine stepping in as Acting MD, and Gnuxie as Finance chair
(two highly active and committed people), that situation is changing. 

There's a lot to do here, as the Foundation is still struggling from a
resources perspective, and still can't cover the bare minimum requirements. So,
I feel the Board has a key role to play in ensuring that Foundation proposals
are given appropriate scrutiny, balanced with the need to help get the
Foundation to a sustainable place. Having a functional Finance committee makes
that possible, and I'm grateful to the folks participating there for their
time.

## Governing Board Meeting Minutes April 2026

As usual, I'm posting a lightly-edited version of the minutes from the meeting
below for your reading pleasure... or you can skip to my conclusions at the
end.

* Introductions & admin
* Committee reports
  * Community
    * Events: Full speed on conference prep, looking at location, CFP, sponsorship
    * Web site: new members, improved processes & daily ops, running well
    * Decentralisation WG: already running but needs a Board sponsor
  * Finance and fundraising
    * Shared that the Foundation is still struggling from a resources perspective & still can’t afford the bare minimum requirements

    * Committee is full of very busy people, could be blocking some things

      * Proposal to merge with the Website WG as some overlap in terms of outputs

  * Governance
    * Core committee feels a bit large. Still building up momentum, topics are now coming and more WGs will likely spin out members.
    * Looking at how to measure (loosely) some for form of impact/effectiveness
    * Working with SCT & Guardians on documenting technical strategy
    * Server/project maturity: attempting to provide ways for projects to report facts
      * We also want transparency in how decisions are made
* Foundation Memberships update
  * Some new members but definitely need more.
  * We can help by promoting membership - explaining the benefits and explaining positive experience of the GB. 
* Premium accounts: big influx of users around Discord time. No more than usual people leaving after that, which is good news.
* Discussions
  * Public annual report
    * Lots support & thanks for getting this done
    * Plenty of discussion, especially around the finance figures and in-kind contributions
    * Discussion around the need to clarify the distinction between Element & Foundation further, and also link to the overall technical strategy (see note about scrutiny and independence above)
* Board activity levels
  * With elections coming up, we took a look at how to make sure we're communicating expectations around the Board members engagement properly, as well as making sure we're giving appropriate recognition to them for work done.
  * Clarity on what skills the Board needs when people are considering standing may help
  * Reminder that Board members can step down if they feel they need to make space for more active folks.
* Closing remarks

## Future plans / personal reflections

The elections start next month, so obviously I wish all the folks looking for
(re)election the best of luck! We're fortunate to have an active passionate
community, and I want people to bring that energy to the Board and help drive
it even further. If that's you, consider standing!

As you can see, the report is *substantially* longer than last time - more WGs,
but with a better process around reports, we also had longer to discuss wider
topics. I can't claim any credit for the work done, that goes to the folks
getting on with it - but I've often described the process of setting up an
organisation as a [flywheel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flywheel), that
takes a lot of energy to get spinning, but once it is... well, it can be hard
to stop! I'm happy to see that we really are spinning now, as evidenced by the
fact that I can no longer keep up with the day-to-day work across the board
(but hey, that's why we have reports). The process is there, now we just need
more Working Groups to broaden it out.
